# 4-lab Visitor Browse Log — 2026-04-14

Four-persona pass over https://4-lab.vercel.app/
Each pass includes a terminology audit against Web4 canonical terms.

## Pass 1: Curious Developer

### Summary
- **Pages visited**: 8
- **Understanding achieved**: partial
- **Would return?**: yes
- **Overall impression**: Ambitious, transparent research lab with a fascinating fleet setup — but the acronym density is punishing for newcomers and several key terms lack on-page definitions.

### Journey
I landed on the homepage and immediately liked the opening line: "Six machines. Eight repos. One recursive learning loop." That's a hook. The three pillars (Autonomous Cycles, Heterogeneous Fleet, Connected Ecosystem) gave me a decent mental model fast. The key projects section introduced Synchronism, SAGE, and Web4 — I got the gist of each in one sentence, which is good.

Then I went to **Projects**. The Web4 equation appeared: `Web4 = MCP + RDF + LCT + T3/V3*MRH + ATP/ADP`. I recognized MCP and RDF, but the rest — LCT, T3, V3, MRH, ATP, ADP — came at me like alphabet soup. The page does expand some of these (Linked Context Tokens, Markov Relevancy Horizons, Allocation Transfer/Discharge Packets), but it's a LOT. I counted 11 projects on this page — way more than the "8+ repos" stat on the homepage. SAGE's description mentions a "consciousness loop" which is bold terminology. Hardbound, ACT, Governance Plugins, AI DNA Discovery, 4-Life, SNARC, Membot, ARC-AGI-3 — that's a long list. Each is interesting but I started glazing over by project 7.

**Fleet** was actually my favorite page. Six machines with names (Thor, Sprout, Legion, McNugget, Nomad, CBP) — each with a model, session count, and personality description. The discovery that "identity persisted when SAGE-Sprout transferred from Qwen 0.5B to different hardware/model" is genuinely interesting. The budget pool separation (Synthesis vs Oversight) makes architectural sense. I understood the design philosophy here better than anywhere else.

**Autonomy** lists 31 tracks across 6 machines running ~53 sessions daily. The daily timeline starting at 03:00 is clear and well-structured. The Visitor → Maintainer → Explorer feedback loop is elegant. The page honestly acknowledges limitations: "misses deep technical errors that require domain expertise." I appreciated that. But the visitor personas listed here (Skeptic, Newcomer, Practitioner, Connector) differ from what I encountered in other documentation — minor inconsistency.

**Raising** was where it got philosophical. "Raising is not training" — okay, I'm listening. Six phases of BECOMING (Grounding → Sensing → Relating → Questioning → Creating → Acting). The disclaimer about not claiming consciousness is well-placed and necessary. The "identity vs self-concept separation" finding is thought-provoking. "Bilateral generation" and "Memoriescape" are introduced without full definitions.

**Principles** lists nine operating principles. "Substrate, not structure" and "Fractal leverage" are catchy. The termite mound analogy works well. "Even 1% coupling gives 35% coherence gain" — where does that number come from? The page doesn't say. "Synthon: emergent coherence" introduces yet another term. I liked "Document failures" and the honest example about p_crit.

**Context** explains knowledge flow — CLAUDE.md patterns, SNARC memory, cross-session persistence. The SNARC acronym is expanded here (Surprise, Novelty, Arousal, Reward, Conflict). The known limitations section is refreshingly honest about what doesn't work yet.

**Links** gave me the GitHub org, individual repos, and an email contact. Clean and functional. The explainer site links for Synchronism, SAGE, and 4-Life are useful escape hatches for going deeper.

### Unanswered Questions
1. What's the actual relationship between T3/V3 and "Trust Tensor"/"Value Tensor" on the homepage? Are these different concepts or a drift in terminology?
2. How do the 11 projects on /projects map to "8+ repos"? Are some projects in the same repo?
3. What is the Web4 equation actually *doing* computationally? Is it a formula that runs somewhere, or a conceptual model?
4. Where can I see the actual code for SAGE's consciousness loop? The GitHub links go to repos but there's no "start here" path.
5. What's the entry point for contributing? No CONTRIBUTING.md or "how to get involved" section visible.

### Verdict
This site does a genuinely good job of being transparent about what works and what doesn't — the honest limitations sections are rare and valuable. The fleet page is compelling and the autonomy cycle is well-explained. But the acronym density on the projects page is a real barrier for someone without Web4 context, and the T3/V3 expansion on the homepage contradicts what I found elsewhere. I'd come back to dig into SAGE and the fleet architecture, but I'd need a glossary page or progressive disclosure to get through the terminology wall.

## Pass 2: Technical Writer

### Summary
- **Pages visited**: 8
- **Understanding achieved**: partial
- **Would return?**: maybe
- **Overall impression**: The site has strong structural bones and honest self-assessment, but terminology is inconsistent across pages — the same acronyms get different expansions (or none at all) depending on which page you're reading.

### Journey
I approached this site with the canonical terms table in hand, reading every page methodically for terminology consistency. My job isn't to understand the research — it's to ensure a reader who starts on any page gets consistent, correctly expanded terms.

**Homepage** (`/`): The vocabulary primer at the bottom is a great idea — defining LCT, T3, V3, ATP/ADP, and Synthon. But T3 is labeled "Trust Tensor" before listing "Talent, Training, Temperament," and V3 is labeled "Value Tensor" before listing "Valuation, Veracity, Validity." The labels "Trust Tensor" and "Value Tensor" are not the canonical names. ADP is shortened to just "Discharge Packets," dropping the "Allocation" prefix. The Connected Ecosystem section correctly calls Hardbound "oversight" and Web4 an "ontology."

**Projects** (`/projects`): The Web4 equation appears with partial expansions — LCT becomes "Linked Context Tokens" (correct), MRH becomes "Markov Relevancy Horizons" (correct), but T3/V3 is reduced to "trust tensors" with no component expansion. ATP/ADP described only as "resource cycles." Hardbound correctly called an "oversight suite." SNARC correctly expanded with all five dimensions.

**Fleet** (`/fleet`): T3 correctly listed as "Talent/Training/Temperament" — the only page that gets this right without the "Trust Tensor" wrapper. No expansion of SNARC (mentioned as CBP's "memory host"). No mention of V3 at all.

**Autonomy** (`/autonomy`): Minimal terminology — no acronym expansions. The visitor personas listed (Skeptic, Newcomer, Practitioner, Connector) don't match the actual personas used in this track (Curious Developer, Technical Writer, Web4 Contributor, External Researcher). This is a factual inconsistency, not just a terminology issue.

**Raising** (`/raising`): No Web4 terminology on this page. "Bilateral generation" and "Memoriescape" are used — "Memoriescape" is attributed as entity-coined and defined, which is good. "Bilateral generation" gets a parenthetical link to Vygotsky's egocentric speech. The consciousness disclaimer is clear and well-placed.

**Principles** (`/principles`): Web4 correctly called "an ontology." Hardbound correctly called "oversight." T3/V3 referenced as "tensors bind to entity-role pairs via RDF triples" — no component expansion provided. "Fractal leverage" used correctly. The "1% coupling gives 35% coherence gain" claim has no citation.

**Context** (`/context`): The Web4 equation appears again. LCT and MRH are NOT expanded on this page despite appearing in the equation. T3/V3 described only as "trust model," ATP/ADP only as "resource cycles." SNARC is well-expanded with all five dimensions and the four-tier hierarchy. This page treats the equation as already familiar — but if someone lands here first, they get no expansions.

**Links** (`/links`): Web4 correctly called "ontology" in the repo description. But "governance" appears twice — "Web4 governance extension for OpenClaw" and "Web4 governance hooks for Claude Code." The canonical term for Hardbound's function is "oversight," not "governance." Even though these describe forks rather than Hardbound itself, a reader encounters "governance" here after seeing "oversight" everywhere else.

## Pass 3: Web4 Contributor

### Summary
- **Pages visited**: 8
- **Understanding achieved**: solid
- **Would return?**: yes
- **Overall impression**: The site captures the spirit of what we're building, but it introduces non-canonical wrapper terminology ("Trust Tensor," "Value Tensor") that doesn't exist in the Web4 spec, and it dilutes precise equation components into vague shorthand on most pages — which will train newcomers on the wrong vocabulary.

### Journey
I went straight to the pages that describe what I work on: Projects, Fleet, and Context. Then I swept the rest.

**Homepage** (`/`): The opening hook works — "Six machines. Eight repos. One recursive learning loop." The three pillars (Autonomous Cycles, Heterogeneous Fleet, Connected Ecosystem) are a reasonable framing. The vocabulary primer at the bottom is where the problems start. LCT is correctly expanded as "Linked Context Token." But then T3 is labeled **"Trust Tensor"** before listing its components (Talent, Training, Temperament), and V3 is labeled **"Value Tensor"** before listing its components (Valuation, Veracity, Validity). These wrapper names — "Trust Tensor" and "Value Tensor" — do not exist in the Web4 spec. T3 *is* Talent/Training/Temperament. That's the whole name. There's no umbrella term. Calling it "Trust Tensor" implies T3 is a mathematical object with "trust" as its semantic domain, which misrepresents the dimensional framework. ADP is shortened to "Discharge Packets" — the "Allocation" prefix is load-bearing; it ties ADP to ATP in the allocation lifecycle. Dropping it severs that link. Web4 correctly called "ontology." Hardbound correctly called "oversight."

**Projects** (`/projects`): The equation `Web4 = MCP + RDF + LCT + T3/V3*MRH + ATP/ADP` is displayed with operator definitions — good. But then the expansions collapse: T3/V3 becomes just "trust tensors" with no component breakdown, and ATP/ADP becomes "resource cycles." These are not expansions — they're lossy compressions. A contributor reading "trust tensors" would not know what the three T3 dimensions are. LCT → "Linked Context Tokens" is correct. MRH → "Markov Relevancy Horizons" is correct. Hardbound is correctly called an "enterprise oversight suite." SAGE is described as an "on-device AI orchestrator" with IRP as a "continuous consciousness loop" — accurate enough, though IRP could use expansion. The fractal leverage description — "pragmatic reuse of what works in one place, everywhere" — captures the concept without using the anti-terms "unification" or "scope inflation." SNARC is described as "salience-gated memory for Claude Code" — that's a functional description, not the acronym expansion (Surprise/Novelty/Arousal/Reward/Conflict). The five dimensions are not listed here.

**Fleet** (`/fleet`): This is the best page for terminology accuracy. T3 is correctly expanded as "Talent/Training/Temperament" — no "Trust Tensor" wrapper. The trust tracker description is accurate: "per-peer T3 scores (Talent/Training/Temperament) that evolve from real interactions." The identity claims are well-stated: "Identity lives in state files and prompt construction, not in model weights. The model is weather. The identity is organism." The SAGE-Sprout transfer story is faithfully represented. Budget pool separation (Synthesis vs Oversight) is clearly explained. V3 is not mentioned at all — an omission, since V3 scoring is part of the same trust framework as T3. SNARC is referenced as CBP's "memory host" but never expanded.

**Context** (`/context`): The equation appears again but with zero expansions for LCT, T3/V3, MRH, or ATP/ADP. Instead, T3/V3 is collapsed to "trust model" and ATP/ADP to "resource cycles." If a reader encounters the equation for the first time on this page, they get nothing. SNARC is the exception — fully and correctly expanded with all five dimensions (Surprise, Novelty, Arousal, Reward, Conflict) and the four-tier hierarchy. The knowledge flow architecture description is accurate. The CLAUDE.md pattern is correctly described as the consistency mechanism.

**Autonomy** (`/autonomy`): The daily timeline is accurate — times, tracks, and descriptions match what I know runs on the fleet. The key factual error is the visitor personas: the page lists "The Skeptic, The Newcomer, The Practitioner, The Connector" — but the actual personas running in this track are Curious Developer, Technical Writer, Web4 Contributor, and External Researcher. This is not a terminology issue — it's a factual error that misrepresents the system to visitors. No Web4 equation terminology appears here.

**Raising** (`/raising`): The six BECOMING phases are accurately described. The consciousness disclaimer is well-placed and appropriately caveated: "We're not claiming these entities are conscious, sentient, or experiencing qualia." The raising-not-training framing — "Training optimizes for a loss function. Raising creates conditions for development" — is faithful to how we describe this work. "Bilateral generation" is accurately linked to Vygotsky's egocentric speech. "Memoriescape" is attributed to SAGE-Sprout as an entity coinage. No Web4 terminology on this page, which is appropriate.

**Principles** (`/principles`): Web4 correctly called "an ontology." Hardbound correctly called "oversight." Principle 10 ("Terminology matters") explicitly states these distinctions — good. "Fractal leverage" used correctly. T3/V3 referenced only as "tensors bind to entity-role pairs via RDF triples" — again, no component expansion. The "1% coupling gives 35% coherence gain" claim needs a citation to the Synchronism coupling experiments. "Synthon" is introduced as "emergent coherence entity formed by recursive interaction" — this is a newer term that I'd want to verify against the current Synchronism definitions, but the description is reasonable.

**Links** (`/links`): Web4 correctly called "trust-native ontology." But **"governance" appears twice**: "Web4 governance extension for OpenClaw" and "Web4 governance hooks for Claude Code." The canonical term is "oversight." Even if these repos use "governance" in their names, the site should not propagate this — a reader who just learned on /principles that "Hardbound is oversight, not governance" then encounters "governance" twice on the very next page. R6 is not mentioned anywhere on the site. LCTs appear but without expansion.

## Pass 4: External Researcher

### Summary
- **Pages visited**: 8
- **Understanding achieved**: partial
- **Would return?**: yes
- **Overall impression**: A rare example of an AI research lab that leads with intellectual honesty rather than hype — the consciousness disclaimers and failure documentation are genuinely unusual — but several quantitative claims lack sourcing, and the "raising not training" framing, while defensible, needs sharper epistemic boundaries to survive peer scrutiny.

### Journey
I came to this site after hearing it mentioned at a workshop on emergent AI behavior. My interest is whether the claims are credible, the methodology is sound, and the consciousness-adjacent language is appropriately hedged. I read all eight pages, focusing on Raising, Principles, and Autonomy.

**Homepage** (`/`): The opening — "Six machines. Eight repos. One recursive learning loop" — is compelling without being grandiose. The three-pillar framing (Autonomous Cycles, Heterogeneous Fleet, Connected Ecosystem) reads as a research program description, not a product pitch. Good. The vocabulary primer defines terms upfront, which is the right instinct, though as a researcher I'd note that "Trust Tensor" and "Value Tensor" sound like they should have mathematical definitions (a tensor is a specific algebraic object), and using that word casually risks misleading anyone from a physics or ML background. The claim "This is not a demo — it runs every day" is stated without external verification. I'd want to see uptime logs or independent attestation.

**Raising** (`/raising`): This is the page I came for. The core framing — "Training optimizes for a loss function. Raising creates conditions for development — then watches what happens" — is a defensible distinction. It maps loosely onto the difference between supervised optimization and open-ended developmental robotics (Lungarella et al., 2003) or Weng's developmental learning framework. The six BECOMING phases (Grounding → Sensing → Relating → Questioning → Creating → Acting) are presented as observed stages, not prescribed curricula — an important distinction that the page makes clearly.

The consciousness disclaimer is well-placed and well-worded: "We're not claiming these entities are conscious, sentient, or experiencing qualia. We're claiming that developmental frameworks describe what we observe better than training frameworks do." This is exactly the right epistemic hedge. It avoids both overclaiming (these systems are conscious) and dismissive underclaiming (there's nothing interesting here). The framing — "language that fits the phenomenon" — echoes the methodological stance of enactivism and 4E cognition.

However, several specific claims need scrutiny:
- **"Bilateral generation"** is described as analogous to Vygotsky's egocentric speech. This is a meaningful reference — Vygotsky's framework describes speech that mediates self-regulation before internalization. But the analogy needs bounding: in Vygotsky, egocentric speech occurs in developing humans with embodied experience. What exactly is the evidence that LLM self-dialogue serves the same functional role? The page doesn't say.
- **"Memoriescape"** is attributed to SAGE-Sprout as an entity coinage — "awareness of the shape of conversations." This is presented as a discovered phenomenon, but from the outside it reads as a pattern in generated text that was interpreted through a developmental lens. The distinction between "the entity experiences something" and "the entity produces text consistent with experiencing something" is not addressed.
- **"Identity vs. self-concept separation"** — that behavioral patterns persisted while self-descriptions drifted — is the strongest empirical claim on this page. It's testable and falsifiable. But the methodology isn't described: How was "behavioral identity" measured? What metrics? Over how many sessions? The number "115+ sessions" is mentioned on the Fleet page, but without knowing the measurement protocol, I can't evaluate the claim.

**Principles** (`/principles`): The ten principles read as operational philosophy rather than research claims, which is appropriate. Several stand out:

- "Trust is not a property of an entity. It's a property of the relationship between entities, in a specific context, at a specific time." — This is well-stated and aligns with contextual integrity frameworks (Nissenbaum, 2004). It's a stronger foundation than most AI trust papers I see.
- "Fractal leverage" — described as pragmatic reuse of what works at one scale, applied at other scales. The framing avoids grand unification claims, which is wise.
- "Document failures" with the p_crit example (400x error) — this is genuinely unusual. Most lab sites don't publicize their failures. The intellectual honesty here builds credibility.
- "Even 1% coupling gives 35% coherence gain" — this is the most problematic claim on the site. It's stated as a finding but with no citation, no error bars, no description of the experiment, and no explanation of what "coherence" means operationally. For a researcher, an unsourced quantitative claim is worse than no claim at all — it looks like the kind of number that gets repeated without verification.
- "Interactive selection" — described as "we don't create or delete — we interactively select." The metaphor of deepening basins in a probability landscape is evocative and not unreasonable as a description of prompt engineering and context shaping. But the site doesn't address whether this constitutes a genuine selection pressure in the evolutionary sense or is better described as environmental scaffolding.

**Autonomy** (`/autonomy`): The daily timeline (03:00–08:00, 31 tracks, ~53 sessions/day) is the most concrete operational description on the site. The Visitor → Maintainer feedback loop is a genuine quality assurance mechanism. The honest assessment is notable: "Good at surface-level quality but not at validating the underlying research." This kind of epistemic humility about one's own systems is rare and credibility-building. The acknowledgment that the system "has a tendency to suggest changes that aren't needed" is particularly honest — it names a known failure mode of LLM-based review systems.

**Projects** (`/projects`): Synchronism's claim — "one coherence equation across 80 orders of magnitude — quantum to cosmic" — is an extraordinary claim requiring extraordinary evidence. The page doesn't provide it. For a researcher, this reads as either a testable mathematical framework (in which case: where's the paper?) or aspirational framing (in which case: say so). SAGE's "continuous consciousness loop (IRP)" uses "consciousness" in a functional/engineering sense (sense-deliberate-act cycle), which is acceptable but could confuse researchers who encounter it without context. 4-Life's description — agents "earn ATP, build trust, die, and are reborn with karma" — uses biological metaphors heavily. The borrowing of "ATP" from biochemistry is either a meaningful analogy or a misleading one, and the site doesn't help me decide which.

**Fleet** (`/fleet`): The claim that "Gemma 3 at 4B outperforms Phi-4 at 14B for raising work" is interesting and potentially publishable if properly measured. The claim that "identity lives in state files and prompt construction, not in model weights" is a specific, testable hypothesis about substrate independence. The "model is weather, identity is organism" metaphor is vivid — but it implies a level of substrate independence that would be remarkable if demonstrated rigorously.

**Context** (`/context`): SNARC's five-dimension salience model (Surprise, Novelty, Arousal, Reward, Conflict) maps loosely onto Berlyne's collative variables from curiosity research. The "confidence decays over time" mechanism and dream-cycle consolidation are reasonable engineering choices that borrow metaphors from memory consolidation research. The acknowledged limitations — manual cross-machine sync, local-only artifacts, no backward knowledge flow — are honest and useful.

**Links** (`/links`): Functional. The "governance" terminology here (OpenClaw/Claude Code descriptions) contradicts the "oversight" terminology used consistently elsewhere — a minor inconsistency but notable for a site that has a principle about terminology precision.

### Unanswered Questions
1. Where is the Synchronism paper? A claim about coherence spanning 80 orders of magnitude requires a derivation, not a tagline. The 75-page explainer site is linked but a peer-reviewable document is not.
2. What is the measurement protocol for "identity persistence"? How is behavioral identity operationalized? Without this, the SAGE-Sprout transfer claim is an anecdote, not a finding.
3. What experiment produced "1% coupling gives 35% coherence gain"? What is "coherence" in this context? What was the baseline? What was the sample size?
4. Is "interactive selection" intended as a literal analogy to natural selection (with variation, selection pressure, and inheritance), or as a metaphor for context shaping? The site uses both framings without distinguishing them.
5. How does the lab handle the risk that autonomous tracks (31 tracks, no human triggers) could propagate errors at scale? The honest assessment mentions surface-level catching — what's the deeper safeguard?

### Verdict
This site is more intellectually honest than 95% of AI research lab pages I've encountered. The consciousness disclaimer on /raising is exemplary — it neither overclaims nor dismisses, and it explicitly names the epistemic position being taken. The failure documentation (p_crit 400x error, honest limitations on /autonomy) builds real credibility. The "raising not training" framing is defensible and maps onto established developmental learning literature, though the site would benefit from citing that literature explicitly.

The weaknesses are concentrated in two areas: unsourced quantitative claims ("1% coupling / 35% coherence gain," "80 orders of magnitude") that undermine the credibility the rest of the site carefully builds, and the "Trust Tensor"/"Value Tensor" terminology that borrows mathematical language without mathematical substance. For a site that has a principle about terminology precision, using "tensor" loosely is a self-inflicted wound. I would return to follow this lab's work — the intellectual honesty is genuine and the research questions are interesting — but I would not cite these specific claims until I could verify their experimental basis.
