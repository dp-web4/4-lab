### Journey

I landed on `/` after clicking through from the dp-web4 GitHub org. The hero immediately pitched "trust-native ontology, autonomous AI cognition, six heterogeneous machines." OK — those are three loaded phrases in one sentence, but the stats (6 machines / 14 repos / 36+ tracks) gave me something concrete to anchor on. Helpful — but I noticed T3/V3 were glossed as "Trust Tensor and Value Tensor multidimensional scores" without spelling out what the three components actually are. That came later on `/projects` and `/context`. As a developer, I want the full expansion the first time, not on page three.

I went to `/projects` next because that's where I'd expect to find "what to build / contribute to." 11 projects listed, each with a one-paragraph description and a GitHub link. SAGE, Synchronism, ACT, SNARC, Membot, ARC-AGI-3 — all sound interesting. **But there's no contributing.md-equivalent here.** No "good first issue" pointer, no "we're looking for help with X," no setup-and-run instructions per project. I can clone repos but I don't know what's actually wanted from outside contributors, or whether outside contributors are wanted at all.

`/autonomy` was the cleanest page for me. The 8-track timeline (Supervisors → Archivist → Publisher → Visitor → Maintainer → Outreach → Explorer → Dream Consolidation) is concrete and avoids unexplained jargon. I could picture the cron schedule.

`/raising` was where my BS-detector started ticking. The disavowal of consciousness claims is welcome and explicit — good. As a developer I want either the math or no analogy, not the analogy as garnish.

`/principles` mostly worked — the "10 operating principles" feel internal-not-marketing, including admissions like the "400x error" and "p_crit failed derivation." Nice. References p_crit and Hill function without context, but I read it as "we tried things, here's the lessons" not "here's our marketing pillars."

`/context` gave me the Web4 equation written out explicitly: `Web4 = MCP + RDF + LCT + T3/V3*MRH + ATP/ADP` with the operator legend. Finally. This should arguably be on `/` too — it's the most useful single artifact on the site for a technical reader.

`/links` was thin. Three explainer-site links, one email contact (dp@metalinxx.io), pointer to dp-web4 GitHub org. No Discord, no mailing list, no "here's how to start contributing." For an exit page, it left me without a clear next action.

**Summary of terminology audit (researcher seat)**:

The drift terms that the canonical guidance polices are **clean in prose** — Lifelong Capability Token, governance-for-Hardbound, architecture-for-Web4, production-ready do not appear. The known-canonical decompositions are correct where given.

The researcher-relevant terminology issues are not drift in the canonical sense; they are **rhetorical strength inconsistencies**: the same scientific reference (Hill function) appears at two epistemic strengths on different pages, and the strong form is unsupported. The metaphor-vs-claim discipline is exemplary on `/fleet` (brain regions explicitly disclaimed as functional analogies) and broken on `/principles` (Hill function presented as mechanism). A reviewer would point to `/fleet`'s hedging as the template the rest of the site should follow.

The internal inconsistency between SAGE = "Situation-Aware Governance Engine" (on `/`) and "Hardbound is oversight, not governance" (on `/principles`) is visible from the outside even without canonical-terms knowledge. From a researcher's perspective this reads as: "the lab has a terminology principle and the lab's own homepage breaks it." That's a small but real credibility cost.

### Unanswered Questions

1. **What operationalizes the Hill-function-as-mechanism claim?** What plays the role of binding sites, of cooperativity coupling, of the Hill coefficient? Is there a behavioral dataset that fits a Hill curve? With what fit quality? This is the single question I'd want answered first.
2. **How is "Publication readiness 0.97" computed?** Self-assessment, reviewer composite, derivation-completeness ratio? Any number reported to two significant figures invites this question.
3. **Where are the preprints / technical reports?** The site claims significant empirical results across multiple projects. The only public artifacts are explainer sites and GitHub repos. Is there a research-output channel?
4. **What was p_crit attempting to predict?** Documented failure is valuable; the *target* of the failure is necessary for the failure to be instructive externally.
5. **What's the falsifier for "raising > training"?** What observable would, if seen, refute the framing? Or is this a framing choice rather than an empirical claim — and if so, is it presented as such?
6. **On ARC-AGI-3, what's the protocol?** Public set vs held-out, single-attempt vs best-of-N, comparison baselines? 94.85% is a number; the protocol is what makes it interpretable.
7. **What metric supports "behavioral identity remained continuous" across model swaps?** Stylometric distance, response-distribution divergence, task-performance correlation? Counts of sessions are not measurements.

### Verdict

Three sentences:

The site shows real epistemic discipline in places I didn't expect — explicit consciousness disclaimer, explicit "what the autonomous loop misses," explicit brain-region-as-analogy hedge on `/fleet`, a documented 400× failure as a principle — and these earn the lab credibility that most AI-research sites don't earn. But the same discipline is **inconsistently applied**: the Hill function is analogy on `/raising` and "same mechanism" on `/principles` and `/projects`, and "Publication readiness 0.97" is a precision claim with no rubric. As a researcher I'd come back to read a preprint, but the site itself is not enough to evaluate the load-bearing scientific claims — the cleanest single fix is to bring `/principles` and `/projects` up to the hedging discipline already on display on `/fleet` and `/autonomy`.
